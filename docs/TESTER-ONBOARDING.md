## Part 1 — Get the app installed *(takes ~3 minutes)*

### Step 1 — Open this link on your Android phone
```
https://play.google.com/apps/testing/com.storeyinfra.app
```
*(I'll send this in your WhatsApp message — just tap it.)*

### Step 2 — Tap "Become a tester"
The page asks you to confirm you want to join the beta. Tap **Become a tester** → **Accept**.

### Step 3 — Open Play Store and search "Storey"
Now the app shows up *for your account only* (it's a closed test — invisible to everyone else).

You may need to wait 5–10 minutes for the search to find it. If you see *"Error DF-DFERH-01"* — wait 30 minutes and try again. That error means Play Store is still updating your account's view.

### Step 4 — Tap Install
The app downloads. ~3 MB. Opens like any other Play Store app.

---

## Part 2 — Your first 5 minutes in the app

### Step 5 — Sign up

You have two paths. **Most testers should use Google sign-in** — it's the fastest and most reliable.

**Option A (recommended) — Google sign-in:**
Tap "Continue with Google" → pick the same Gmail you sent us. Done.

**Option B — Email + password:**
Tap "Create account." Enter company name, your name, email, password. You'll receive a confirmation email (from `noreply@storeyinfra.com` — check spam if it doesn't appear).

*(A third option — phone-number login via SMS — is available but currently in preview. SMS delivery in NE India can be slow or unreliable. We recommend Google or email signup until the SMS path is fully stable.)*

### Step 6 — Create your company
First-time contractors are asked: *"What's your company name?"* Enter the real one — it appears on Work Orders and reports later.

### Step 7 — Add your first site
Tap **Sites → Add site**. Enter the site name (e.g. *"NH-37 Furkating"*), location, and the team assigned. Takes 60 seconds.

### Step 8 — Try one thing — file a daily log
Tap **Daily Logs → New log**. Add: workers present, work done today, weather, one photo. The photo opens the camera; capture, attach, save. That's the core workflow.

## A note on data security

Your data is stored on Amazon's Mumbai servers (Supabase) — the same backbone that runs banking apps in India. Each company's data is fully isolated by encryption and access rules. **No other contractor, no outsider, not even Storey staff** can see your data without your permission.

Once "Your data, your Drive" ships (v1.2), every night Storey will save a complete backup of your data to your own Google Drive. You'll have a copy you can touch, copy to a pen drive, store on your PC — exactly like Tally's data folder. If Storey ever disappears (it won't), you keep every record.
