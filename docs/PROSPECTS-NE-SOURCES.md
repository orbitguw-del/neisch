# NE-India prospect sources — contractors & developers

*Compiled 2026-06-02. For building a legitimate outreach list for Storey
onboarding/trials. Honest caveats below — read them first.*

## ⚠️ Read first
- **No fabricated email list here.** These are the real, verifiable *sources* you
  pull names + contacts from. Made-up/scraped personal emails are inaccurate and a
  **DPDP Act 2023** problem.
- **Channel matters more than the list.** Per your own roadmap, NE contractors are
  **WhatsApp-first** and respond to **referrals**, not cold email. Cold email works
  better for **developers** (real-estate firms with offices/email) than for small
  contractors.
- **Warm > cold.** Three intros from the BAI Guwahati chapter or a referral beat a
  500-name cold blast. Use the directories to get *names*, then reach them the way
  this audience actually responds.

## Recommended approach (after production ships)
1. **Warm first:** BAI Guwahati chapter + Guwahati Construction Directory (BIC) +
   CREDAI Assam — ask each for a member-intro or to present Storey. One association
   relationship = many warm leads.
2. **Referrals:** every tester/pilot (Arun) → ask for 2 contractor intros. Customer
   N+1 from customer N is your cheapest, highest-converting channel.
3. **Directories for names → WhatsApp for contractors, email for developers.**
   Pull names from the govt registries / D&B; reach contractors on WhatsApp (their
   channel), developers by email (theirs).
4. **Keep cold outreach small + targeted** — not a blast.

## Compliance + deliverability (don't skip)
- **DPDP Act 2023 / TRAI commercial-communication rules** — unsolicited bulk marketing
  has constraints in India. Keep outreach targeted, relevant, with a clear opt-out.
- **Domain reputation** — do NOT cold-blast from `noreply@storeyinfra.com` before the
  **SPF/DKIM warm-up** (your P3 TODO) — a cold blast can get the domain flagged and
  break your transactional email (password resets, invites). Warm the domain first.
