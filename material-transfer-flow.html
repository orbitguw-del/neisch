Material Transfer — Flow

Storey · revised inter-site transfer process
The Store Keeper — central material oversight
• The Store Keeper sees all material stock & movement across every site and the warehouse — a tenant-wide view, like the Contractor (today they only see their assigned sites).
• They watch stock levels everywhere and can initiate a transfer to rebalance material between sites.
• A Supervisor cannot initiate a transfer — only a Store Keeper or Site Manager can. The Supervisor executes the dispatch once asked.
Transfer lifecycle — 4 stages
1
STORE KEEPER / SITE MANAGER
Initiate
Raises a transfer request — from-site, to-site, material and quantity to move. Status: Initiated.
2
SUPERVISOR (from-site)
Prepare & confirm dispatch
The request appears on the from-site Supervisor's dashboard. They fill the dispatch detail — actual quantity, vehicle number, challan/LR — and confirm. Status: Prepared.
3
STORE KEEPER / SITE MANAGER
Approve dispatch
Reviews the prepared transfer and signs it off. Status: Approved (or Rejected — sent back to the Supervisor).
4
RECEIVING SITE
Accept on receipt
The receiving site accepts the delivery, noting the quantity actually received (discrepancy handling as today). Stock arrives at the to-site. Status: Received.
Statuses
Initiated Prepared Approved Received Rejected
When stock actually moves
• From-site stock decreases at Stage 2 — Prepared, when the Supervisor confirms dispatch (the material has physically left the site).
• To-site stock increases at Stage 4 — Received (quantity actually received).
• Stage 3 — Approval — is a sign-off check; the material is already in transit. A rejection there flags the transfer for a correction/return rather than un-sending it.
• Any gap between sent and received is recorded as a discrepancy — same as the current receipt flow.
• Every movement is written to the material ledger with a running balance.
Activity log — who did what
Every stage stamps the person and the time — so a transfer carries its own audit trail:
• Initiated by — name + time
• Prepared / dispatched by — Supervisor name + time
• Approved (or rejected) by — name + time
• Received by — name + time + quantity received
The transfer detail shows this as a step-by-step activity trail — no more "we can't tell who moved it".
Stored as initiated_by, prepared_by, approved_by, received_by (each with a timestamp) on the transfer row.
⚠️ Scope: this changes (a) the Store Keeper's data access — tenant-wide for material tables; (b) the transfer table — 4 statuses instead of 2, plus dispatch-detail fields; (c) adds a Supervisor dashboard item; (d) RLS for who can initiate / prepare / approve / accept. A real redesign of the transfer module — best built as its own set of phases.
