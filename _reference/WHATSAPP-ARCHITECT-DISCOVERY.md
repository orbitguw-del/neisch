## Why each question matters

| # | Question | What the answer tells us |
|---|----------|--------------------------|
| 1 | BOQ format | Whether the contractor ever sees a BOQ at all, and if so what shape |
| 2 | BOQ fee + price point | The willingness-to-pay anchor for Storey's pricing |
| 3 | Quantifier Pro / plugin use | Whether SketchUp files in this region carry quantity data — usually NO |
| 4 | Where contractors keep BOQ | The import path Storey should build (Excel paste? Photo upload? Manual?) |
| 5 | Sample SketchUp file | **Highest leverage.** 30 minutes inspecting a real file beats 10 surveys |

---

## Expected answers (most likely scenario)

If our hypothesis is right, the responses will be:

1. *"Yes, Excel."* OR *"Sometimes I make a rough one, but contractor usually makes his own."*
2. *"Included for big projects, separate for small (₹5k–15k)."* OR *"Contractor hires a QS."*
3. *"No plugin. I do quantities in Excel by hand from the model."*
4. *"Excel on their laptop. Sometimes Tally."*
5. *"Sure, I'll share."* OR *"Need to ask client first, but ok."*

If that's roughly the answer pattern, **Tier 1 manual BOQ entry is the right product**, Tier 3 stays as enterprise customisation, and Tier 2 (Excel paste) becomes a strong v1.5 candidate after 5 paying customers.
