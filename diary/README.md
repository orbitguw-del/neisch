# Storey — founder diary

> The journey is the asset. This folder is where the walls and the workarounds get
> remembered, before they fade.

## How this works

One markdown file per day. Filename = `YYYY-MM-DD.md`. Write in your own voice
or have Claude help shape three sentences into the template — both are fine.

## The rules

1. **5 minutes maximum.** No 30-minute introspection sessions. Founders who
   journal at length quit by week 2.
2. **Three questions, no more.** Compression forces insight.
3. **Honesty over polish.** If today sucked, write "today sucked." Don't varnish.
4. **Walls and workarounds are the gold.** When something fights you and loses,
   write it down. That's the part future-you (and future investors) will value.
5. **Skip days are fine.** Don't gamify a streak. Show up when it matters.

## The template

```markdown
# YYYY-MM-DD — Day N of 90-day roadmap

## What I shipped today
-

## What blocked me (or tried to)
-

## What I learned (about the product / customer / myself)
-

---
Mood: 🟢 / 🟡 / 🔴
MRR: ₹X · Testers: X/12 · Open bugs: ?
```

## Wall-and-workaround entries

When you hit a real wall and break through it (today's camera saga is the
template), capture it as a story arc inside the day's entry:

```markdown
## Wall: <one-line summary>
**What broke:** what the user saw
**What I assumed:** my first theory
**What was actually true:** the real root cause
**How I fixed it:** the path that worked
**What I learned:** the rule that'll save future-me from repeating this
```

The good ones become content later — blog posts, conference talks, investor
narrative pieces. Don't hide them in commit messages.

## Privacy

This folder is in the repo but **never published**. If you ever open-source
Storey, exclude `diary/` from the open-sourced branch. Keep it private. It's
your record, not marketing.
